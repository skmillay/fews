About This Visualization

The Famine Early Warning Systems Network (FEWS NET) is a leading provider of early warning and analysis on acute food insecurity.

The Integrated Phase Classification (IPC) acute food insecurity scale classifies areas according to a five-phase scale of increasing severity.

Acute food insecurity is a complex outcome, typically driven by a combination of economic, natural, political, and/or social factors. The nature, location, magnitude, and duration of complex drivers, including climate, conflict, and markets & trade inform classifications.

This visualization begins to explore the relationship between these data in East Africa. Select highlighted food security classification (FSC) units to view a time series.
